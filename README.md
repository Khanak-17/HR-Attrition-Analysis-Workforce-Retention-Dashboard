📊 HR Attrition Analysis & Workforce Retention Dashboard

Python · SQL · Power BI · Excel

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, salary distribution, and employee retention trends.

The dashboard helps HR teams and business managers monitor important workforce KPIs, identify attrition patterns, and make data-driven decisions using dynamic visualizations, slicers, and filters.

📸 Dashboard Preview

Interactive Power BI dashboard showing employee attrition trends, department-wise analysis, workforce demographics, salary insights, and retention performance.

📊 Dashboard Features
👥 Employee KPI Overview

The dashboard contains interactive KPI cards for:

Total Employees
Attrition Count
Attrition Rate
Average Salary
Average Age
Average Years at Company

These KPIs dynamically update based on selected filters and slicers.

🏢 Department-wise Attrition Analysis

Analyze employee attrition across departments such as:

Human Resources
Research & Development
Sales

Helps identify departments experiencing higher employee turnover.

📈 Attrition by Age Group

Visualizes attrition trends among different age categories:

18–25
26–35
36–45
46–55
55+

Provides insights into workforce retention by age demographics.

💼 Job Role Analysis

Displays attrition distribution across various job roles including:

Sales Executive
Research Scientist
Laboratory Technician
Manager
Healthcare Representative

Helps identify roles with maximum employee turnover.

🎓 Education & Job Satisfaction Insights

Includes interactive charts for:

Attrition by Education Field
Job Satisfaction Levels
Work-Life Balance Analysis

Useful for understanding employee engagement and satisfaction patterns.

💰 Salary & Income Analysis

Provides insights into:

Monthly Income Distribution
Salary Slab-wise Attrition
Compensation Impact on Retention

Helps identify salary-related attrition trends.

🕒 Years at Company Analysis

Analyzes employee retention based on experience and tenure.

Helps determine whether attrition is higher among new employees or experienced staff.

💡 Key Insights
Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition rate
Sales department experiences maximum employee turnover
Lower salary slabs have higher attrition percentages
Employees with lower job satisfaction are more likely to leave
Work-life balance significantly impacts employee retention
